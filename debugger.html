Debugger

Ah... instead of playing, could you join the Dev team to fix our game?
Game Design
UXUI Design
3D Modeling
Challenge
I participated in the GMTK (Game Maker’s Tool kit) 2023 Game Jam with a few of my friends to create a fully functioning game from 0 to 100 within 48 hours.

With the prompt of “Roles Reversed” we liked the idea of the player being the one working on the game, instead of playing it. So, instead of playing through the game as the protagonist, players have to take on the role of the developer, and squash bugs instead of enemies.
Ideation
The moment we got the prompt “Roles Reversed”, we started to list out various ideas to ideate on.

We eventually chose a theme where the player “plays” as a game developer, and has to “fix” our bad game.
Inspiration
For the world, we wanted to make it look like it was still “in progress” while being a complete game. We took inspiration from games like Superhot, along with black and white volumetric sketches of buildings.
For errors, we used familiar “no texture” textures to convey that something was glitching.
To really immerse the player , we added text notification and chat UI to make it seem like they are communicating with the rest of the game making team.
Storyline/Plot
After some workshopping on the story, we decide that the player would be helping us (the team making this game) to fix bugs in time for us to submit for this game jam (pretty meta.. I know).

The game starts off with a cut scene with messages from us saying we need help, then by exploring the map, the player collects items that help them “fix” the level and help us complete the game in time for submission.
Submission available on Windows here
Takeaways
Scoping was a big issue. We kept having fun ideas that we wanted to execute, but with only one person programming and working on unity, it was difficult to balance fun ideas and realistic expectations. It was our first game jam as a team, so understanding each of our abilities and limits would help the next time we’re under a time crunch.

Overall, it was a very fun experience to make a complete working game within 48 hours.
